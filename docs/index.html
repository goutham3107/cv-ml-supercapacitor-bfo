BiFeO3 ML Pipeline

1. ML-Based Prediction of CV Behavior

Focus: Zinc and Cobalt-substituted Bismuth Ferrite (BiFeO3).

Accelerating supercapacitor research by replacing physical trial-and-error with high-accuracy predictive AI.

2. The Core Problem

  • Traditional Cyclic Voltammetry (CV) testing is time-intensive and heavily resource-dependent.
  • Physical synthesis of multiple dopant variations creates severe bottlenecks in next-generation battery research.

3. Project Objective

  • Develop a full-stack Machine Learning pipeline capable of predicting CV current curves based purely on physical input parameters.
  • Reduce laboratory turnaround times from days to milliseconds.

4. Material Focus: Bismuth Ferrite

  • BiFeO3 offers unique multiferroic properties highly suitable for advanced energy storage.
  • We analyze how specific Zn and Co substitutions affect its electrochemical performance and overall specific capacitance.

5. Dataset Architecture

  • Total Volume: 218,200 data points.
  • Training Split: 216,200 rows to train the algorithms.
  • Validation Split: 2,000 completely unseen rows for rigorous real-world testing.
  • Inputs tracked: Potential, Scan Rate, Oxidation state.

6. Feature Engineering & Distribution

  • Text-based states (Oxidation/Reduction) were perfectly transformed into binary format for mathematical processing.
  • Applied standard scaling to ensure varying unit magnitudes do not skew the neural network weights.
  • The dataset represents a perfectly balanced electrochemical cycle.

7. ML Pipeline Overview

  • A multi-model ensemble approach was engineered to capture different types of non-linear relationships.
  • Combining deep learning with decision-tree logic ensures we do not over-index on a single mathematical philosophy.

8. Base Model 1: Artificial Neural Network

  • Architecture: Deep learning model with two hidden layers (100 nodes, 80 nodes) using ReLU activation.
  • Strengths: Excels at identifying highly complex, hidden patterns in continuous datasets.
  • Training Dynamics: Notice the rapid convergence in early epochs, minimizing Mean Squared Error (MSE) without overfitting the validation data.

9. Base Model 2: Random Forest

  • Architecture: Ensemble of 40 decision trees capped at a maximum depth of 11.
  • Strengths: Provides exceptional stability and robust resistance to dataset noise.
  • Feature Insight: The algorithm mathematically confirmed that 'Potential' and 'Scan Rate' are the dominant drivers of the electrochemical current.

10. Base Model 3: XGBoost

  • Architecture: Gradient boosting framework (100 estimators, 0.1 learning rate).
  • Strengths: Sequentially corrects errors of previous trees, providing highly accurate fine-tuning.
  • Boosting in Action: The RMSE drops sharply in distinct steps as each subsequent boosting round aggressively targets outlier data points.

11. Model Strengths & The Meta-Model

  • Every model has trade-offs. To solve this, we stacked the predictions of the ANN, RF, and XGBoost into a RidgeCV Regressor.
  • This "Boss Model" dynamically weights the underlying algorithms to maximize final accuracy.

12. Training Accuracy Metrics

  • Base Models Average R²: 97.50%
  • Meta-Model Final R²: 97.85%
  • The stacking technique successfully outperformed any individual algorithm during the training phase.

13. Real-World Validation (Unseen Data)

  • Tested against an entirely unseen dataset recorded at 60 mV/s.
  • R² Score: 99.66%
  • RMSE: 0.000456
  • The tight linear clustering in the residual scatter plot proves the model generalized perfectly.

14. Specific Capacitance Verification

  • The ultimate test of the model is calculating the physical energy storage capacity.
  • Experimental: 0.01798 F g⁻¹
  • Predicted: 0.01732 F g⁻¹
  • The model accurately derived the physical capacity with less than a 4% margin of error.

15. Future Work & Conclusion

  • Integration: Connect the ML model directly to electrochemical workstations via API for real-time testing feedback.
  • Expansion: Scale the feature architecture to accommodate other transition metal oxides.
  • Conclusion: AI can reliably replace preliminary physical CV testing, massively accelerating supercapacitor research.
